Power interest Matrix

The Power Interest Matrix is a tool used to map stakeholders based on their level of interest in a project and their power to influence its outcomes.

In this framework, game developers and players show high interest due to their direct involvement and experience, but have limited influence over strategic decisions. Researchers combine high interest with high power, as they shape core concepts, methodologies, and long-term directions. Policy makers hold significant power through governance and regulation, while their interest in day-to-day development and use remains relatively low.

Ari Kessler

37 | Principal Developer | HoloForge
Pain: Unreliability and inconsistency in current interaction tools. Long waiting times, broken workflows, and missed deadlines.
Gain: Dramatically reduce support tickets, onboarding friction, and creative bottlenecks for non-technical users.
Frustrations: Unpredictable tools make critical complexity "boring magic," making debugging impossible.
Goals: Build the world's first intuitive, trustworthy "human-to-digital" platform.

Elara Novak

42 | Associate Professor | CISL
Pain: Struggles with the "black box" nature of current AI toolsets; lack of transparency in logic or digital creative tool workflows.
Gain: Provide clear logic and explainability in AI-driven systems to researchers, making it easier to build on and innovate.
Frustrations: Deeply frustrated by "hype" narratives that overshadow robust research and empirical "mind-reading".
Goals: Establish ethical standards for digital agency/AI interaction and create frameworks for "human-in-the-loop" prototyping.

Sofia Lagardere

54 | Principal Policy Architect | European Creative Technologies Directorate
Pain: The "Wild West" environment of high-speed AI creation and the lack of existing policies/guidelines.
Gain: Establish robust legal frameworks to provide certainty for creators and artists, protecting intellectual property.
Frustrations: The speed of technological advancement often outpaces the ability to create nuanced and effective regulation for the public good.
Goals: Ensure the fair use of AI-assisted creativity, protecting intellectual property and cultural values while encouraging innovation.

Luca Fernandes

28 | Artist | Self-employed
Pain: Fears that AI tools might replace human artistry, leading to a loss of craft and cultural identity.
Gain: Empowerment from intuitive AI tools that allow creation without deep technical knowledge.
Frustrations: Dislikes overly complicated interfaces, high costs of software, and AI tools that feel opaque or unapproachable.
Goals: Use AI tools to explore creative ideas more quickly and efficiently, making art more accessible to all.
