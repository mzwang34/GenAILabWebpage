public stack

Foundation

Core goals and principles: transparency, openness, sustainability.
Ethical and social value: fairness, accessibility, and environmental responsibility.
Multi-stakeholders are involved Financial support: hybrid mode including public funding, community contributions, and commercial partnership.

Design

Professional developers: design and maintain fundamental framework.
Creators and artists: propose feature requirements, test and refine tools, and optimize creative workflows.
Normal users: use for educational or personal purposes, provide public supervision, and give feedback to both creators and developers.
Iterative process: updates can be made without breaking existing systems, allowing continuous improvement and modular flexibility.

Tech

Infrastructure: distributed cloud and edge computing network providing real-time rendering, AI-driven content generation, and scalable collaborative environments. Supports high-performance GPUs and neural processing units for large-scale virtual worlds. Biological signals are transferred into neural parameters as input for the world model. World rendering and logic are generated in the cloud, reducing local computation requirements.
Equipment: compatible with a wide range of devices, from high-end PCs and AR/VR headsets to lightweight devices, ensuring accessibility for everyone.
Platform and formats: standard format for assets. Ensure interoperability between clients, plugins, and other engines, and allow content transfer.
Application: include rendering, physics simulation, AI-based content generation, animation, and collaborative editing. Applications support both real-time gameplay and creative prototyping.
Test tools and plugins: automated CI/CD pipelines for testing engine modules, plugins,and world packages. Allow community contributions and iteration.
Local deployment: Lightweight client packages support offline or low-bandwidth scenarios. Users can run simplified simulations locally while syncing with cloud for rendering and logic updates. Designed for classrooms, remote collaboration, or personal experimentation.
Security: End-to-end encryption for user data and biological signals. Access control for collaborative worlds and assets provenance. Ethical content filters prevent harmful or inappropriate AI-generated content.

Citizen perspective

Accessibility: the engine is designed for all users, supporting multiple input methods including voice, gesture, and emotion. Lightweight clients and offline deployment ensure that educational institutions, remote areas, and casual users can participate without high end hardware.
Rights: creators maintain ownership of their worlds and assets. Contributions are tracked through data and rewarded via fair sharing models or community recognition. Users have full control over their data and can manage privacy settings.
Market: community-driven marketplace for assets, plugins, and world packages. Players and creators can buy, sell, or license content.
Community governance: Decisions on engine standards and moderation are made through the multi-stakeholder governance. The community can propose and vote on protocol updates, content policies, and ethical guidelines.
